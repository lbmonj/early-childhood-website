Introduction: Helping Every Child Grow and Thrive at Forest Friends Discovery Preschool

At Forest Friends Discovery Preschool, we believe every child is a unique learner with boundless potential. We create safe, joyful spaces where children explore, play, and build strong social-emotional skills alongside a lifelong love of learning. Rooted in the natural world and inspired by the kindness of forest friends, our inclusive community nurtures curiosity, empathy, and joy in discovery.

Our approach honors the whole child—academically, socially, emotionally, and physically—while embracing diversity and the power of connection. We partner closely with families to support each child’s journey, ensuring every little explorer feels seen, valued, and empowered. Together, we cultivate a community where kindness blooms and every child can grow into their best self.

Impact: The Importance of Early Childhood Education and Intervention

Early childhood is one of the most critical periods in a child’s development—a time of rapid brain growth when early experiences shape the foundation for learning, behavior, and lifelong well-being. Research consistently shows that children who participate in high-quality, inclusive early education are more confident, socially capable, and academically prepared when they begin kindergarten (National Institute for Early Education Research, 2021).

At Forest Friends Discovery Preschool, we believe early intervention is not just beneficial—it’s essential. When developmental delays or differences are identified early, we can respond proactively, embedding playful, targeted supports into everyday routines and interactions. This approach helps children build skills before gaps widen, supporting more positive long-term outcomes in learning, relationships, and mental health (Guralnick, 2011).

Inclusive classrooms like ours give children with both typical and atypical development the chance to grow and learn side by side, fostering empathy, cooperation, and shared success. Our research-based, developmentally appropriate practices are tailored to each child’s unique needs—and grounded in strong, respectful partnerships with families. Together, we lay the foundation for every child to thrive.

School Readiness: Meeting Every Learner Where They Are

At Forest Friends Discovery Preschool, we recognize that school readiness isn’t one-size-fits-all. It’s a holistic concept that goes beyond letters and numbers to include emotional regulation, communication, physical coordination, social skills, and cognitive development. Every child develops at their own pace and brings unique experiences shaped by family, culture, and language.

To truly understand each child’s current level and needs, we use a variety of developmentally appropriate, culturally responsive tools. These include developmental milestone checklists, play-based assessments, anecdotal records, family interviews, and ongoing teacher observations during natural routines and play. This comprehensive approach allows us to appreciate the whole child in their real-world context.

For example, a child may avoid group play or have trouble making eye contact at school, showing signs of atypical social development. However, their family reports that at home, the child plays cooperatively with siblings and engages in familiar routines with ease. These differences are important. They may reflect the child’s comfort level, sensory needs, or difficulty adjusting to new environments. Our staff takes time to understand these dynamics by listening to families, observing across contexts, and providing consistent, gentle support to help the child feel safe, connected, and ready to engage at school.

By involving families in the assessment process and honoring cultural and linguistic diversity, we ensure every child is seen and supported. We believe readiness is not a fixed trait but a capacity nurtured through individualized, play-based learning and supportive experiences that build confidence and prepare each child for a successful transition to school and lifelong learning.

Learning Influences: How Culture and Family Shape Learning

At Forest Friends Discovery Preschool, we understand that every child arrives with a rich history of culture, language, and family experiences that profoundly shape how they learn and grow. These differences are not barriers, but strengths that enrich our community. We celebrate the unique ways children express empathy, independence, and understanding—whether it’s through family routines in a multigenerational home or culturally specific social interactions. For example, a child from a multigenerational household might show compassion or autonomy in ways that differ from typical classroom behaviors, and recognizing this helps us respond effectively. The child from a multigenerational home might be used to comforting a grandparent by bringing them water, food, or sitting quietly beside them without being prompted. In the classroom, instead of saying “Are you okay?” to a crying peer (a typical classroom response), the child might bring the peer a toy or silently sit beside them as a way of offering comfort—less verbal, more action-based compassion.

Our curriculum embraces diverse cultures by inviting families to share their traditions, languages, and stories through songs, books, and classroom celebrations. We use tools such as dual-language storybooks, visual supports, and inclusive communication strategies to bridge language gaps and support all learners. For example, a typically developing child from a Spanish-speaking family may confidently sing Spanish songs during circle time, showing strong language skills in their home language. A child with a speech delay from the same background may participate by clapping or using picture cards. By including Spanish music and visuals, we support both children—reinforcing language, building confidence, and honoring their culture.

By building strong connections with families and deeply understanding each child’s background, we tailor our teaching to meet the diverse needs of children with both typical and atypical development. This inclusive approach ensures every child feels understood, valued, and empowered to thrive in our classroom community.

Social-Emotional Learning: Helping Children Build Relationships and Resilience

Social-emotional learning (SEL) is at the heart of everything we do at Forest Friends Discovery Preschool. Using evidence-based programs like Conscious Discipline and the Pyramid Model, we nurture essential skills such as empathy, self-regulation, cooperation, and problem-solving. These strategies are designed to support all children, including those with exceptionalities, by incorporating visual aids, social stories, repetition, and sensory supports.

SEL is embedded seamlessly into daily routines—greeting each child by name, using visual emotion charts, modeling empathy, and discussing feelings through stories. For example, during a daily “feelings check-in,” children identify emotions using picture cards, allowing a child with language delays to participate fully by pointing instead of speaking.

We also integrate social-emotional learning into play and group activities. When children build a block tower together, they practice sharing, turn-taking, communication, and conflict resolution. Calm-down corners, sensory tools, and movement breaks provide children with strategies to regulate their emotions and manage transitions smoothly.

By fostering social-emotional skills early, we equip every child with the tools they need to build friendships, handle challenges, and thrive both academically and socially throughout their lives.

Collaboration: Partnering with Families and Professionals

At Forest Friends Discovery Preschool, we believe collaboration is essential to supporting every child’s growth and development. Families are the child’s first and most important teachers, and we actively include them as partners in decision-making, goal-setting, and learning. To foster strong communication and trust, we hold family-teacher chats twice a year (or more frequently by parent or teacher request) where we discuss progress, share strategies, and co-create individualized goals tailored to each child’s needs.

We invite families to participate in classroom events and activities, such as sharing family traditions or helping develop behavior support plans, strengthening the connection between home and school. When parents raise concerns—like speech delays—we work together to try targeted strategies both at school and at home before making referrals to specialists.

Our collaboration extends beyond families to include early intervention providers, speech and occupational therapists, special educators, and service coordinators. Our team regularly attends IEP and IFSP meetings and maintains open communication with outside professionals to ensure consistent support across all settings.

Transparency is a core value at our preschool. We provide weekly updates, photos, and progress reports through a family communication app, keeping families connected to their child’s daily experiences and achievements. By creating a strong, coordinated network of support, we help every child thrive in a nurturing, inclusive community.

Key Features of Our Program

  • Inclusion and Diversity: These programs welcome children with typical and diverse needs, including disabilities, fostering environments where every child is valued and respected. Children learn together through play, exploration, and social interaction, which builds empathy, acceptance, and understanding of differences.
  • Developmental Approach: Programs follow developmentally appropriate curricula addressing cognitive, physical, language, social-emotional, and academic skills. Teachers individualize instruction based on each child’s abilities and progress, often using authentic observations and assessments.
  • Play-Based Learning: Play is central, supporting active learning and social interactions. Music therapy and hands-on activities further aid development.
  • Family Involvement: Strong partnerships with families are encouraged through open communication, conferences, classroom participation, and support, recognizing family engagement as critical to child success.
  • Specialized Support: Programs often collaborate with clinical and therapeutic services (speech, occupational, physical therapy) embedded within the school day to support children with additional needs.
  • Philosophy of Inclusion: Rooted in national standards (DEC/NAEYC), inclusion means full participation, belonging, and access to quality education for all children. Inclusive practices benefit children with and without disabilities.
  • Holistic Development: Centers focus on the whole child, including health, character, life skills, and social-emotional foundations, ensuring children are prepared not just academically but also emotionally and socially for school and life.
